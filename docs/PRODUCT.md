# happyhotel AI Revenue Manager

## Product Purpose

A next-generation AI Revenue Manager product for independent hotels. The AI acts as a hired employee — monitoring pricing, executing actions, escalating to human RM experts (Marc, Lisa) when confidence is low. The system is the hotel's outsourced commercial department: AI + human experts, one continuous workflow.

## Register

product

## Users

**Primary: Hotel Owner / General Manager ("The Missing Middle")**
- Mid-sized independent hotels, small hotel groups, €2M+ annual revenue
- Has NO dedicated full-time Revenue Manager
- Wants outcomes and operational relief, NOT RM expertise
- Makes decisions based on: money, growth, required action — never on KPI jargon
- Communicates in German; "du" form
- Uses the product on desktop (morning review) and mobile (quick decisions on the go)

**Secondary: Human RM Experts (Marc, Lisa — happyhotel staff)**
- Appear inside the product in the same conversation stream as AI actions
- Handle complex/ambiguous situations that AI escalates

## Strategic Principles

1. **Bringschuld, not Holschuld** — system pushes briefings to the hotelier; dashboards are drilldown, never the front door
2. **Ergebnisse vor Daten** — translate every metric into money: "Du hast 12.400 € verdient" not "RevPAR Pickup +0.4"
3. **Explainability** — every price, every action has a visible "Why"
4. **Hybrid is visible** — Marc and Lisa appear in the same stream as AI, not in a separate support channel
5. **Trust is graduated** — per-dimension trust levels (price magnitude, horizon, restrictions, channel, group size), not a binary on/off toggle
6. **Tier = AI seniority** — hiring metaphor: Revenue Manager (€8/room) → Senior (€12) → Director (€20)

## Tone

- German, "du" form
- Confident but warm — like a trusted colleague, not a corporate tool
- Plain language: no RM jargon without translation
- Action-oriented: "Genehmige" / "Erkläre" / "Zeig mir mehr"

## Anti-references

- No brutalist/heavy black border wireframe aesthetic — that was Wurf 3 and is rejected
- No 12-column wall of numbers (the old Preistabelle)
- No jargon-heavy dashboards (RevPAR, ADR, STLY without translation)
- No separate "support" or "chat" feel for the human experts — they live inside the product

## Existing Capabilities (to surface in UI)
